Editorial Standards

Phavai exists to make product decisions clearer. These standards define how we collect evidence, rank products, use shopping links, correct mistakes, and protect user trust.

Last updated April 20, 2026 Ranking independence Source transparency Corrections welcome

Ranking independence

BestPick Scores are calculated from evidence signals before shopping links are considered. A product should not move up because it has better availability, stronger merchant coverage, or a more convenient retailer relationship.

Evidence quality

We prioritize category-specific expertise, hands-on testing, credible creator reviews, owner sentiment, recency, and recurring patterns across sources. A single enthusiastic source is not enough to create high confidence.

Visible tradeoffs

Every recommendation should show who it is best for and who should avoid it. Fit, durability, cost, complexity, and owner complaints belong near the score rather than buried after the buying button.

Shopping links

Shopping links are provided for convenience. They may point to Amazon, brand stores, or other retailers. Link availability does not affect BestPick Score, consensus, signal strength, or editorial conclusions.

Updates and corrections

Pages show last-updated and source-verified dates where appropriate. If a source becomes outdated, a product changes materially, or a better source is available, Phavai should update the dataset and regenerate the affected page.

What Phavai avoids

We avoid generic filler, unverifiable social claims, hidden ranking logic, unsupported star ratings, and recommendation language that sounds more certain than the evidence deserves.

Phavai protects trust by separating evidence, scoring, and shopping links.
