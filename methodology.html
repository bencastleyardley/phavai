How BestPick Scores Work

Phavai ranks products by evidence quality, source-weighted performance, and transparent confidence indicators. Shopping links are not scoring inputs.

Expert reviews: 40% YouTube reviews: 30% Reddit sentiment: 30% Social excluded until auditable

1. Evidence collection

Each product starts with evidence items: expert reviews, creator reviews, owner sentiment, official specifications, and source verification dates. Evidence is stored as structured data so rankings can be regenerated instead of manually rearranged.

2. Source tiers

Reviewer tiers are category-specific. A specialist trail-running lab does not automatically become a trusted office-chair source. Tier 1 sources carry the strongest weighting, Tier 2 sources add real-use context, and Tier 3 sources capture owner patterns.

3. Channel scores

Evidence rolls up into Expert, YouTube, and Reddit channel scores. Each evidence item is adjusted for tier, freshness, relevance, and sample strength before the channel score is calculated.

Channel formula: sum(evidence score x evidence quality weight) / sum(evidence quality weights).

4. BestPick Score

BestPick combines the active channel scores using category weights. The current default is Expert 40%, YouTube 30%, and Reddit 30%. If a channel is missing, available channels are normalized rather than filled with a guess.

BestPick formula: sum(channel score x category channel weight) / sum(available category channel weights).

5. Consensus

Consensus measures agreement across weighted channel scores. A product can have a high BestPick Score but lower consensus if experts praise it while owner sentiment repeatedly flags fit, durability, or support issues.

6. Signal strength

Signal strength reflects evidence count, source diversity, freshness, reviewer tier quality, sample strength, and channel depth. It is shown separately so weak evidence cannot hide behind a clean score.

7. User-adjustable rules

Review pages let readers adjust Expert, YouTube, and Reddit weighting in the browser. This recalculates rankings without changing the underlying evidence, making the ranking easier to challenge and personalize.

8. Why social is excluded

Broad social media can be useful, but it is noisy and hard to audit without a reliable API-backed pipeline. Phavai excludes it until collection is reproducible enough to deserve influence.

9. Shopping links

Shopping links may point to Amazon, brand stores, or other retailers for convenience. They do not affect source scores, BestPick Score, consensus, signal strength, ranking order, or product conclusions.

What makes a recommendation fail

A recommendation fails if it hides meaningful tradeoffs, uses unverifiable evidence, overstates certainty, or ranks a product because the link is more convenient. Trust is the product.
